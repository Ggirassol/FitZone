London Marathon 2023 route map: Where the race is, road closures list in full and the start time, explained

This will be the 43rd running of the London Marathon, with the event returning to its traditional April slot.

The 2023 London Marathon takes place this weekend, with 50,000 amateur and professional athletes preparing to take on the 26.2-mile route around the capital.

This will be the 43rd running of the London Marathon, with the event returning to its traditional April slot. It was moved to October during the Covid-19 pandemic, and remaining there in 2022.

Here is everything you need to know about the race, from starting times to the route map and road closures.

When is the 2023 London Marathon?

The marathon takes place on Sunday 23 April, with the wheelchair and professional races kicking off first. The following timings have been announced:

  1. 8.50am - Elite wheelchair men's and women's races
  2. 9.00am - Elite women;s race
  3. 9.40am - Elite men's race and mass start

The mass participation event is expected to begin from around 10am, with a sequence of start waves to allow the course to clear up ahead. Everyone is expected to have crossed the starting line by 11.30am.

Participants are given one of four different assembly areas - red, green, blue or yellow - from which they will begin. If you are running, this information should be in your registration email, which you should have received before Friday 14 April. The colour of the digits on your bib number will match your assembly area.

Professional athletes usually need just over two hours to complete the course but many amateurs will take three to five hours. Participants are given a maximum of eight hours to complete the course.

What is the London Marathon route?

The marathon course starts in Greenwich and Blackheath, with runners assigned one of three start lines.

They will merge after three miles, passing some of London's most famous landmarks before finishing on The Mall, opposite Buckingham Palace.

The first stretch takes the runners through Charlton, Woolwich and Greenwich, and they will pass the Cutty Sark at mile six.

They will then progress through Deptford, Surrey Quays, Rotherhithe and Bermondsey, before crossing Tower Bridge shortly after mile 12.

From here it's back east towards Limehouse, a big circuit around Canary Wharf and the Isle of Dogs, before doubling back through Shadwell to Tower Gateway.

The final miles take the runners along the River Thames, past St Paul's Cathedral and through Blackfriars and Temple.

They will pass the London Eye and the Houses of Parliament before coming to a stop by Buckingham Palace.

Spectators can view the marathon along the route. It is also being shown live on BBC One, and can be streamed on BBC iPlayer.
